## What We Know
- The standing witness benchmark survived the extended symbolic fairness ladder, including stronger symbolic basis, shared-atlas, residual-atlas, dual-atlas, bilinear, cubic, quartic, and quintic review families.
- The result transferred across five materially different bounded transfer families.
- The result did not transfer uniformly; `braid`, `staggered-binding`, and `fan-in consensus` are now explicit failure boundaries.
- Current evidence supports an internal mechanism claim, not a hardware, NISQ, or external superiority claim.

## Recommendation
### Continue
- Preserve the line as an internal mechanism program.
- Use the current standing benchmark and transfer portfolio as the internal reference set.
- Keep theory, screening, and memo-level candidate design active.

### Do Not Do Now
- no hardware reopening
- no publication push
- no default transfer-family proliferation
- no same-family symbolic escalation by default

## Reopen Conditions
Only reopen execution if a new candidate passes all of the following:
- transfer intake template
- survivor-vs-braid structural screen
- deep-reindex non-compressibility review
- explicit bounded fairness contract

## Program Value
The line remains valuable because it now provides:
- one hardened internal benchmark
- multiple bounded transfer successes
- multiple clear failure boundaries
- one reusable intake and screening process for future candidates

## Program Risk
The main risk is process drift: reopening low-value execution branches without enough structural justification.

## Board-Level Decision
- `Continue at low intensity`
- `Preserve internally`
- `Do not escalate externally`
- `Do not reopen hardware`

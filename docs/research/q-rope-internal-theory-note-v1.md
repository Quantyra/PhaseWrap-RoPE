## Proposition 1
A frozen symbolic family over declared summaries, bounded chart families, and bounded low-order interactions is insufficient to match the standing witness on the benchmark task.

### Support
- The witness stayed ahead of the original frozen symbolic basis.
- It also stayed ahead through the stronger symbolic-family ladder including shared-atlas and dual-atlas variants.
- It remained ahead even after bounded higher-order dual-atlas transition families up through quintic-plus.

### Interpretation
This does not prove impossibility for all symbolic families. It establishes insufficiency for the specific bounded symbolic families that were defined, frozen, and audited in this repo.

## Proposition 2
The witness advantage is not purely benchmark-local.

### Support
- The path-local transfer family survived a bounded nuisance and structural hardening cycle.
- The loop-closure transfer family also survived a bounded nuisance and structural hardening cycle.

### Interpretation
This does not establish broad generalization. It establishes bounded transfer across two materially different relational task families.

## Proposition 3
The most plausible current explanation is latent-conditioned relational response structure.

### Support
- The symbolic families only observe declared summaries and bounded expansions thereof.
- The witness includes latent-conditioned response channels that are not exposed directly as declared symbolic features.
- The strongest symbolic families tested still did not close the gap.

### Interpretation
This is a structural explanation, not a hardware explanation and not a formal proof.

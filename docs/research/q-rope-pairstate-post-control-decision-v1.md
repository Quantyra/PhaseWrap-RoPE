# Q-RoPE Pair-State Post-Control Decision v1

## Decision
- `STOP EXPANSION`
- `RETURN IMPLEMENTED BRANCH TO ARCHIVE POSTURE`
- `PRESERVE THE REPRESENTATION IDEA`

## Why
The sector-permutation control answered the right question.

It showed:
- the aligned packet is extremely strong
- the permuted packet weakens sharply
- most of the signed offset-gap advantage disappears once direct sign alignment is broken

That is enough to reject expansion under the current bar.

## What the evidence now supports
The pair-state direction is not empty.

It supports one narrow claim:
- explicit relational sector structure can generate strong synthetic separation when the sector aggregation is aligned with the task label rule

It does not support the stronger claim we would need for expansion:
- that the mechanism has already demonstrated a robust relational advantage that survives a direct alignment challenge

## Branch status
### Implemented branch
- `closed`
- no broader synthetic family
- no benchmark expansion
- no remote execution

### Memo-level pair-state direction
- `preserved`
- still the strongest representation-level idea in the archive
- may be reconsidered later only under a stricter restart brief

## Correct interpretation
This is not a failure in the same sense as the earlier null branches.

It is a validity-limited partial success:
- positive under aligned synthetic conditions
- not strong enough to justify continuation as an active implementation track

## Reopen condition
Do not reopen this branch with small tweaks.

Only reopen if a future restart brief can show, on paper, a materially different way to:
- preserve explicit relational structure
- avoid direct label alignment
- and retain sector-resolved measurement without collapsing back into a global proxy

- `useful archive result`

## Failure-Boundary Check
- Distinct from `braid` because the task is not dominated by crossing-order compression.
- Distinct from `echo-resolution` because repeated recurrence is replaced by counterfactual replacement pressure.
- Distinct from `selector-arbitration` because the selector is not choosing between symmetric candidates; it is testing one handoff against a later counterfactual override.

- coarse handoff state is near-null by construction,
- within-state target variation remains nontrivial,
- latent handoff diversity is preserved across paired contexts,
- the decisive relation depends on persistence against a counterfactual alternative rather than on additive local summaries.

## Narrow Theory Statement
The evidence supports the following internal statement.

> A witness-style relational representation can preserve useful latent-conditioned structure beyond a long audited sequence of bounded symbolic summary families, provided the task family remains non-compressible under deeper reindexing.

This is narrower than any claim about hardware, NISQ utility, or practical superiority on real transformer workloads.

## Why The Criterion Fits The Evidence
### Standing benchmark
The standing benchmark survived progressively stronger symbolic families even after the symbolic basis was widened in a controlled, auditable way.

### Transfer survivors
The preserved transfer families survived bounded nuisance and structural hardening cycles without collapsing into the corresponding bounded symbolic controls.

### Braid boundary
`braid` failed specifically where deeper structural perturbation exposed a symbolic recovery path. That is exactly the failure mode described by the non-compressibility criterion.

## Internal Consequence
The witness should not be described internally as ōbetter because quantumö or ōbetter because phase alone.ö

The more defensible internal framing is:
- the witness appears to encode latent-conditioned relational state
- the audited symbolic families we allowed cannot reconstruct that state on the preserved benchmark and survivor-cluster transfers
- when a task family compresses under deeper reindexing, the witness advantage disappears

## What This Does Not Prove
- no proof of universal transfer
- no proof against all possible symbolic families
- no hardware or NISQ claim
- no external/publication-grade RoPE claim

## Best Internal Use
Use this note to anchor future internal discussion whenever a new branch is proposed. The question should be:
- does the candidate require latent-conditioned state that should remain non-compressible under deeper reindexing?
If the answer is weak, the branch should not reopen.
